The end of AI amnesia.

AI models forget. We build attention that keeps memory exact — and reads only what matters — at any context length.

eu·mne·sia — from Greek eu (good, healthy) + mnēsis (memory). In clinical language, eumnesia is memory functioning as it should: nothing lost, nothing blurred. The opposite of amnesia — and our standard for machine intelligence.
THE PROBLEM

Efficient AI trades memory for speed.

Compress the past into a fixed-size state, and a model starts forgetting facts it read moments ago. Keep the past in full and recall is perfect — but the cost of attention grows quadratically, until long context becomes unaffordable.

The field calls this the recall wall. Every long-context system today picks a side of it.

WHAT WE'RE BUILDING

Memory that doesn't fade. Costs that don't explode.

Our first architecture keeps a model's memory exact and learns where to look — reading a small, chosen fraction of the past while preserving the recall of full attention. Sub-quadratic, end to end.

It is in active development. Details arrive with the paper.
